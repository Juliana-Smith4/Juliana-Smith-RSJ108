5 Essentials to Prepare for an Interview

Time needed: 1-2 weeks
Helpfulness rating: 95% helpful

Are you nervous about conducting your first interview as a journalist? Follow these steps to feel prepared and confident in your interview, and walk away with a great story!

  • Things You'll Need

  • iPad or Laptop
  • Notetaking system
  • Recording Device
  • Interview subject

  1. Set up an interview.

    Contact your subject either in person, via email, or on the phone. Schedule a time that works for you to meet. Ideally, this is in person so that you can capture more of their facial expressions and body language as well as their answers. If you are able to do an in-person interview, do it in a place that makes your subject comfortable (i.e. their office) or somewhere that fits your story (i.e. a sports field).

  2. Research your subject.

    Start by looking up as many basic facts as possible- name, age, birthplace, career. Use resources such as professional profiles or their past works. If possible, talk to people the subject knows to get outside information.

  3. Write your interview questions.

    a. When writing your questions, think about how long you want the story to be as well as the direction you want it to take. Keep questions open-ended for better conversation, and avoid leading or tricky questions. Always remember to ask the subject “Anything else you would like to share with me?” at the end of the interview.

  4. Set up a notetaking system.

    It is important to have two ways of recording in interview. Use a device with a microphone for audio recording, and write down notes using whatever method you like best- typing, notepad, etc. You can use a shorthand while taking notes to keep up with the pace of the interview.

    A quick guide to shorthand notetaking.

  5. Day of the Interview

    Arrive early to meet your subject. Introduce yourself if necessary and make them comfortable before you start the interview. Ask for permission to record the conversation, and give them any other information they may need before beginning the interview.Watch the video below for tips on interviewing from seasoned journalists from NBC!

  6. Good luck and happy interviewing!
